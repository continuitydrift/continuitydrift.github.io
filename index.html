The ship, known as Beacon, was half-buried at the end of an overgrown skid, the heat of its scorching, pre-historic impact having liquified veins of low-melting-point minerals, the wounded surface now holding the crumpled hull fast in a furrow of stone. The shell of its fuselage was broken, revealing coils of corroded wires and crystals. All organic life, at the time of impact, had been vaporized. The humanoid passengers had met a burning or pulverizing end, yet their stories, so complete as to resemble ghosts, haunted the decks and squinted out at the planet with surviving curiosity.

Language is a powerful tool for understanding one another and the world in which we live. I derive professional and personal satisfaction from finding new paths, through words, into accurate and vivid expression. The more transparent language becomes, the more robust an ecosystem of shared information will be allowed to develop.
