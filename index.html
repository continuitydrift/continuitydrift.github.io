the story

Just as the ecosystem was collapsing, dimensional storms started opening portals to alternate earths.

In one of these realities, I was the kind of person that created websites. In this branch of the multi-verse, I had no actual knowledge of how to do so, but was haunted by vauge visions of sharing online information.

meta

I just got another notice that my monthly hosting bill for the New Language Studio site came due. It would make more sense, probably, to focus my energies there, updating and improving the crude html page I've designed to advertise my proofreading, editing, and writing services.

I'm drawn, though, to the idea of cybernetic space earned solely by shouldering into a knowledge of programming, a sort of sneak attack on the info-sphere, a proof of concept that you can have an informational or narrative system that relies on nothing, resource-wise, that can migrate, Romani style, from digital place to place.

I have fears about if I will even be able to push this verion of the index to the repository and have it be displayed. The truth is I paid someone else fifteen dollars to fix this website when I couldn't get Jekyll to build it with a theme applied, and now I'm nor sure I can build it more on my own without breaking it.

There is some complicated stuff about the difference between local files and the directory that gets included in pushes to the remote repository. I'm just cudgeling with crude stone tools at the surface of this site, not elegantly making use of the architecture.

Notes

I'm finishing up a year teaching English in Mexico. In the future, I would like to get into tech writing, localization testing, and amateur digital art and music.

core

Before all else, I am a writer. A cybernetic exoskeleton of digital technological interests and abilities hides a musculature and organic system of narrative and language.

This website, like others I have created or will create, is a meant to be a node connecting information, a synapse in the hive-mind.

We are variously mediated and reflected masks. It makes sense for me to take the stage as a fiction seller, tending towards the truth instead of profit.
