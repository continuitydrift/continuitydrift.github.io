I opened up the logs like pages of a book. Diachronic slice--not a time slice, not those nested circles, but loosely ruled waves of beige, dark red. Black walnut, my brother says, the most prized wood of Missouri.

Enemies of fly and ill-positioned swings. You have to look, when splitting wood, for where the cracks alreaddy want to form. The same with an idea you are tending to. A bird feeder of language you sit and wait and watch.

Part of me was sad for the death of the trees, their uncrafted rot and decay. Destined for the fire. But I had this chance to read them, to memorize their vivid colors after I had opened them to the sun, before they dried. I could study the scurrying, egg-carrying ants.

I need a camera, I thought, or I could try to simulate those waves in ink. Program a web page to show their layers in parallax. All these things I didn't have the time or resources for, a trail of bugged and broken code. Yet: that moment, and this one, were full fruitions, honor to the various loops of metabolic function. To see things fully, to live, is an art in itself, or the thing behind and obscured by what people think of as art.
